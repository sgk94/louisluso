## Core Differentiator

First to introduce **ULTEM (PEI)** material to the U.S. eyewear market — originally developed by GE for aerospace/medical use. Key properties:
- **30-50% lighter** than conventional frame materials
- Highly flexible
- Heat-resistant

## Market Position

- U.S. eyewear + lens market: ~$21B (2015 figures used in this doc)
- Market has been split between expensive branded frames and cheap Chinese imports for 20+ years
- Growing **mid-price, high-quality** gap — consumers on both ends migrating toward the middle
- Q-Vision sits squarely in this sweet spot

## Strategy & Action Items

### Short-Term (3-36 Months)

**Product Diversification**
- Launch sunglasses line (target: Q1 2019) — previously attempted early on but failed due to low brand awareness; now viable with established customer demand
- Launch children's frames (target: summer 2018) — underserved niche with far fewer competitors

**Sales Force Expansion**
- Scale from 3 reps to **30 reps by 2020** (7-10 new reps/year)
- Direct correlation: more reps = more revenue
- Each new rep produces immediate sales impact given existing brand traction

### Mid-Term (4-6 Years)

**Market Diversification**
- Expand into Europe, starting with **Belgium** as entry point
- Localize products for European market based on Belgium experience
- Build on U.S. distribution foundation before expanding internationally

**New Materials R&D**
- Develop frames using **urethane (TPU)** — comparable to ULTEM in weight and durability but more shatter-resistant, better color adhesion, and superior elasticity
- Position as the next breakthrough material after ULTEM

### Long-Term (7-10 Years)

**EyesnDocs App** (eyesndocs.com)
- Smartphone-based eye exam & diagnosis platform — "virtual optometry office"
- Real-time video consultations with patient's optometrist regardless of location
- Insurance processing built in
- Revenue streams: optometrist membership fees, 5% service fee per consultation, commissions on eyewear/lens orders, advertising

**Brand Diversification**
- Leverage Louisluso brand into adjacent product categories
- First target: **women's face masks / cosmetics** — riding the Korean beauty (K-beauty) globalization trend

## Key Takeaways

1. **Revenue was ~$420K in 2017** with just 3 reps — the growth thesis is entirely driven by scaling the sales force
2. **Revenue scales linearly with rep count** — each rep covers a region and generates immediate reorders from stores
3. **Brand loyalty is building** — existing stores are increasing order volumes and giving Louisluso more shelf space vs competitors
4. **$505K funding** covers supplier debt ($305K) + growth capital ($200K)
5. **Belgium** was the original European entry plan (later pivoted to UK subsidiary in Leicester)
6. **EyesnDocs** and K-beauty brand extension are aspirational long-term plays, not near-term priorities
